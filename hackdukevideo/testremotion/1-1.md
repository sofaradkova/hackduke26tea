# Spinning Toy Problem - Solution

**Source:** 2017 AP Calculus AB Free Response Question 5

## Problem Statement

A company designs spinning toys using the family of functions:

$$y = cx\sqrt{4-x^2}$$

where $c$ is a positive constant. The region in the first quadrant is bounded by the $x$-axis and the graph of $y = cx\sqrt{4-x^2}$ for $x \in [0, 2]$. Each spinning toy is the solid generated when such a region is revolved about the $x$-axis. Both $x$ and $y$ are measured in inches.

---

## Part (a): Find the area of the region R in terms of $c$

The area is given by:

$$A = \int_0^2 cx\sqrt{4-x^2}\, dx$$

**Substitution:** Let $u = 4 - x^2$, so $du = -2x\, dx$, which gives $x\, dx = -\frac{1}{2}\, du$

**Change of limits:**
- When $x = 0$: $u = 4 - 0 = 4$
- When $x = 2$: $u = 4 - 4 = 0$

**Integrating:**

$$A = \int_4^0 c\sqrt{u} \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) du = \frac{c}{2}\int_0^4 u^{1/2}\, du$$

$$A = \frac{c}{2} \cdot \left[\frac{2}{3}u^{3/2}\right]_0^4 = \frac{c}{2} \cdot \frac{2}{3}(4^{3/2} - 0)$$

$$A = \frac{c}{3} \cdot 8 = \frac{8c}{3}$$

### âś… Answer: $A = \frac{8c}{3}$ square inches

---

## Part (b): Find the volume of the solid in terms of $c$

Using the **Disk Method** (revolving about the x-axis):

$$V = \pi \int_0^2 [cx\sqrt{4-x^2}]^2\, dx$$

$$V = \pi \int_0^2 c^2x^2(4-x^2)\, dx$$

$$V = \pi c^2 \int_0^2 (4x^2 - x^4)\, dx$$

$$V = \pi c^2 \left[\frac{4x^3}{3} - \frac{x^5}{5}\right]_0^2$$

$$V = \pi c^2 \left[\left(\frac{32}{3} - \frac{32}{5}\right) - 0\right]$$

$$V = \pi c^2 \cdot 32\left(\frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{5}\right) = \pi c^2 \cdot 32 \cdot \frac{2}{15}$$

$$V = \frac{64\pi c^2}{15}$$

### âś… Answer: $V = \frac{64\pi c^2}{15}$ cubic inches

---

## Part (c): Find the value of $c$ for which the volume equals $2\pi$

Set the volume equal to $2\pi$:

$$\frac{64\pi c^2}{15} = 2\pi$$

Divide both sides by $\pi$:

$$\frac{64c^2}{15} = 2$$

$$c^2 = \frac{30}{64} = \frac{15}{32}$$

Since $c > 0$:

$$c = \sqrt{\frac{15}{32}} = \frac{\sqrt{15}}{\sqrt{32}} = \frac{\sqrt{15}}{4\sqrt{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{30}}{8}$$

### âś… Answer: $c = \frac{\sqrt{30}}{8} \approx 0.684$

## Key Concepts Used

- **Substitution Method** for integration ($u = 4 - x^2$)
- **Disk Method** for volume of revolution: $V = \pi \int_a^b [f(x)]^2 dx$
- **Algebraic simplification** of definite integrals
- **Solving for parameters** in geometric formulas
